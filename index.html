Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

If your helmet displays visible damage, deformation, is over 5 to 7 years old, no longer fits properly, has damaged retention systems, lacks current safety certifications, or has been subjected to extreme usage, it's time to replace it for optimal safety.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding, ensure your bike is thoroughly inspected for any signs of damage or degradation due to prolonged storage to ensure safety.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always wear a properly fitted helmet, obey traffic laws, use hand signals for turns, stay visible with proper lighting and reflective gear, and yield to pedestrians to ensure safe biking.
